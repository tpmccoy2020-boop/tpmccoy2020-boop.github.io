§ 03How I Work

Operating Principle

"Standardize the routine so judgment goes to the exceptions."

Close cycles get messy when the routine isn't documented — so I document it. Reconciliations get a defined tolerance threshold. Controls get a named owner. Audit trails get built on the way in, not reverse-engineered at year-end.

That discipline is why month-end is boring and why auditors leave without findings. It's also what frees time for the work that actually matters: catching exceptions early, pacing cash against forecast, translating ledger activity into decisions leadership can act on. Every dashboard, template, and tie-out in this portfolio exists because a senior finance team shouldn't be spending its judgment on tasks that should have been standardized two cycles ago.

/ 01
Controls before dashboards.

The prettiest report can't rescue a weak reconciliation. I build the tie-out discipline first, then the visualization layer on top.

/ 02
Thresholds over instincts.

Every recurring task gets a documented tolerance, owner, and escalation path. Exceptions go to humans; routine goes to the template.

/ 03
Reporting that drives a next step.

If a dashboard doesn't change what leadership does on Monday, it's decoration. I build for the decision, not the chart.
